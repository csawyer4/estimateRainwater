Rainwater Collection

What Exactly is
Rainwater Collection

Rainwater collection, or rainwater catchment, is a type of green infrastructure. The water that falls onto your roof and goes down the down spout is collected, or caught, in a barrel or cistern. A hose is connected to the bottom of the barrel for convenient watering of your garden, plants, or just your lawn.


Collecting rainwater is important, especially in urban areas. These areas are packed with impervious surfaces including homes, buldings, and roads. Rainwater lands on these surfaces and have nowhere to go but across them, picking up chemicals and debris along the way. This surface runoff then starts puddling, creating areas of flooding, and can even overwhelm stormwater drains and lead to a combined sewage overflow. The surface runoff and sewage then flow right into our rivers and water bodies, reaking havoc on aquatic species like salmon.

Why collect rainwater then? Know you're making a difference in your community and your environment, and save water and money while you're at it!

Community Benefits

How Collecting Rainwater
Benefits the Community

Collecting rainwater reduces urban flooding. This means less ecological damage and less structural damage. Additionally, you can use that rainwater to water your garden, plants, or lawn, saving you money. Working with the environment has been shown to make people happier and healthier.
